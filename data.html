Data Collection

We will collect data using the Spotify web developer API. This API provides access to detailed data such as top tracks, playlists, and genre popularity scores, allowing us to analyze users' listening habits over time. The plan for the data size between 2,000 and 5,000 records, is not too narrow for unbiased and not too overwhelming for the later upload stage of the project. We will also gather seasonal and weather data using the OpenWeather API. We hope to identify the relationship between tracks' popularity and seasonal changes by combining genre popularity with weather patterns within the same timeframe. The data collection process involves retrieving information on top tracks and playlists across various genres, including play counts and listener demographics. ⁤⁤⁤Data will be preprocessed and normalized for accurate comparisons. We will collect data from the Spotify API to obtain metrics on top tracks, playlists, genre popularity scores, and timestamps. This data will be integrated with corresponding weather information from the OpenWeather API, including temperature and weather conditions at the time of music streaming. ⁤⁤This comprehensive dataset will serve as the foundation for our analysis, helping us uncover meaningful insights into the relationship between music and seasonal patterns.
