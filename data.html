Data Collection

We have collected data using the Spotify web developer API. This API provides access to detailed data such as top tracks, playlists, and genre popularity scores, which allowed us to analyze users' listening habits over time. The data utilized was between 2,000 and 5,000 records, which was not too narrow for unbiased and not too overwhelming for the later stage of the project. We also gathered seasonal and weather data using the OpenWeather API. We hoped to identify the relationship between tracks' popularity and seasonal changes by combining genre popularity with weather patterns within the same timeframe. The data collection process involved retrieving information on top tracks and playlists across various genres, including play counts and listener demographics. ⁤⁤⁤Data was preprocessed and normalized for accurate comparisons. We collected data from the Spotify API to obtain metrics on top tracks, playlists, genre popularity scores, and timestamps. This data was integrated with corresponding weather information from the OpenWeather API, including temperature and weather conditions at the time of music streaming. ⁤⁤This comprehensive dataset served as the foundation for our analysis, and helped us uncover meaningful insights into the relationship between music and seasonal patterns.
